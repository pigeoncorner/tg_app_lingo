Research suggests that social media usage may have significant effects on mental health and well-being (Smith & Johnson, 2023). This essay examines the complex relationship between social media usage and mental health outcomes. It is argued that while social media platforms can provide valuable opportunities for connection and self- expression, excessive or problematic use may contribute to negative psychological effects. Research Findings According to a longitudinal study conducted by Thompson and Davis (2023), adolescents who spent more than three hours per day on social media platforms demonstrated significantly higher levels of anxiety compared to those with moderate usage. Furthermore, several researchers have proposed that the type of social media engagement may be more important than the quantity of time spent online. Martinez et al. (2024) suggest that passive consumption of content, such as scrolling through feeds without interaction, may be more detrimental to mental health than active engagement, such as posting content and communicating with friends.
